# I Must Kill — Hit Points & Damage Numbers

## Player Hit Points

A hunter's **Hit Points (HP)** equal **half their Fate score, rounded up**.

| Fate Score | Hit Points |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| 2          | 1 HP       |
| 3          | 2 HP       |
| 4          | 2 HP       |
| 5          | 3 HP       |
| 6          | 3 HP       |
| 7          | 4 HP       |
| 8          | 4 HP       |
| 9          | 5 HP       |

### Typical Starting HP by Array

- **Balanced** (6/6/6/6) — Fate 6 = **3 HP**
- **Specialized** (4/4/8/8) — Fate 8 = **4 HP**
- **Expert** (3/4/8/9) — Fate 9 = **5 HP**

HP totals are intentionally low. Combat is dangerous, and every hit matters.

---

## Player Damage Output

A successful **Attack** (roll 1d10 ≤ Attack Stat) deals:

- **1 damage** on a normal hit
- **2 damage** on a critical hit (natural 1)
- **−1 damage** if the hunter's Insight is too low to perceive the monster's true form

Hunters pick one of Body, Agility, or Focus as their **Attack Stat** at character creation. Stats range from 2–9.

---

## HP Recovery (In and Out of Combat)

| Action          | HP Gained | Notes                                                                                   |
| --------------- | --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dodge (success) | +1 HP     | Roll 1d10 ≤ Agility; also immune to dodgeable attacks until next turn                   |
| Brace (success) | +1 HP     | Roll 1d10 ≤ Body; requires a weapon, armor, or shield; also immune to braceable attacks |
| Grit Teeth      | Full HP   | 1-minute respite; also draw 3 powers; no roll required                                  |
| Night's Rest    | Full HP   | 8 hours sleep in a comfortable place; also resets Insight rerolls                       |

---

## Death & Survival

When a hunter reaches **0 HP**:

- Roll 1d10 against Fate.
- **Success (≤ Fate):** Survive with 1 HP.
- **Natural 1 (critical success):** Survive and restore **all HP**.
- **Failure:** The hunter dies.

---

## Monster Damage (GM Reference)

Monsters deal damage based on their Attack stat and any special abilities listed in their table entry.

- Monster attacks are resolved by rolling 1d10 against its **Attack value**.
    - Roll ≤ Attack value: the monster **attacks** successfully.
    - Roll > Attack value: the monster **Winds Up** — its next successful attack deals **double damage**.
- Monster HP is typically **multiplied by the number of hunters** in the party, scaling the encounter appropriately.

---

## Design Intent

- Hunter HP is small (typically 2–5) to make every combat decision meaningful.
- Successful defensive actions (Dodge/Brace) recover HP, rewarding active defense.
- Attacks deal a flat 1 damage (2 on crit), so stat investment in your Attack Stat is about _consistency_, not scaling damage.
- The death roll (vs Fate) gives a last-chance survival mechanic driven by luck and destiny, keeping individual moments tense.
